The “Self-Confidence & Best-Self” Challenge:

Everyone expects - and needs - you to be Self-Confident and bring your Best Self to the table, all the time, on all levels, as specialist, manager and leader. You are expected to up- skill continuously, unlearn old habits and skills, then re-learn new ones, so that you can perform with unmatching self-confidence.

As you walk your career and life path upward, your organisation will expect you to remain technically proficient in your field, understand ever changing computer and software systems, be emotionally intelligent, spiritually mature, psychologically stable, outstanding communicator, presenter of note, values driven and much more - all this reflecting your levels of Self-Confidence and Best-Self. Welcome to the new reality in our VUCA - volatile, uncertain, complex, ambiguous - world in overdrive and it is only going to intensify.

Hence, you have hopefully realized you need to continuously develop yourself in order to become and remain self-confident, through opportunities provided by your organisation - if you are fortunate - or preferably on your own. Because of the amazing information age we live in, you are able to read articles and books, listen to podcasts and watch videos, learn from others’ examples, receive mentoring, do courses, diplomas, more degrees, attend webcasts and engage other activities to sharpen the saw.

Important Questions to Consider

How do you:

1. Demonstrate that you have engaged in all your continuous development activities that are often readily available - almost prove that you have acquired the necessary skills and knowledge for that next responsibility?

2. How do you demonstrate that you have extracted relevant learnings, skills and knowledge, especially practical application, from all your development activities?

3. Even with formal certificates, diplomas and degrees that promise specific outcomes, how do you show that you have captured, internalized and practiced the relevant learnings from it? We all know that much of the content of these programs arent practically relevant. How do you show that you have prioritized the essentials, because you know what is and will be expected of you in that next role?

4. How do you navigate the transition from specialist to manager and formal leader, then adapt your continuous development activities accordingly?

5. How do you - in todays cost sensitive times - afford financially to continuously develop, upskill yourself real-time (all the time, continuously), so that you can match the real-time world?

Embrace the Journey!

Solution = Me-Vision Academy
