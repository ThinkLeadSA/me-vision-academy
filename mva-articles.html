    It’s time to identify and leverage our Natural Leaders…

    In the vast realm of leadership, the perennial question persists: Are leaders born or made?
    Believe it or not, this is still a relevant question, considering the persistent challenge and need to multiply more quality leaders inside our organisations. Leaders find themselves in different camps regarding this issue, and perhaps, it's worth reflecting on where you stand.

    Camp 1: The Makers
    There are those who staunchly believe leaders are made, advocating for development initiatives. This camp emphasizes the role of nurture over nature, insisting that effective leaders can be crafted through deliberate efforts. Notably, those deriving their livelihood from leadership development passionately champion this theory.

    Camp 2: The Innate Believers
    On the opposite end are leaders who assert that successful leaders are born. This group, often skeptical of development programs, relies on inherent qualities and tends to resist efforts to alter these traits. While not vocally proclaiming their views, it is nevertheless their belief.

    Camp 3: The Compromisers
    Caught in the middle are leaders who privately adhere to the belief that leadership is an inborn trait but publicly align with the development narrative due to external pressures. They navigate a delicate balance between nature and nurture perspectives.

    Camp 4: The Integrators
    Leaders in this camp acknowledge a blend of nature and nurture in leadership formation. Some lean more towards nature, while others emphasize nurture. In general, they recognize that personal life experiences, coupled with carefully crafted cultivated ones, contribute to leadership success.

    Leaders who fall inside camp 4 are able to honestly reflect on their own journey, asserting that cultivated experiences, like planned development programs, played a role but weren’t the main catalysts for their personal growth.
    They recognise the influence of the following factors: luck; painful experiences; failures; successes; upbringing; mentors; osmosis - naturally learning from leaders one is exposed to; reflective coaching sessions; general life opportunities; physical attributes; attitude; schooling system; team-sports experiences; example of parents, significant others; friends; and more.

    The Verdict: Camp 4 Prevails
    I align myself with Camp 4, asserting that leadership excellence is a fusion of inherent attributes and refined skills, mostly acquired through natural life experiences, but complemented by intentional cultivated ones.

    What’s needed:
    More and more what’s needed most urgently is a unique approach - the authentic identification of “naturally” successful leaders, recognizing them exclusively, and empowering them to foster the next generation of successful leaders - enlisting them more actively. This is how you will gain unprecedented momentum in your organisation.

    Identifying Naturally Successful Leaders - How?
    While performance is a key indicator, it actually serves as a green light; it indicates which leaders to zoom in on. Lasting, authentic successful leadership goes beyond results—it’s about the journey to success. Naturally successful leaders lead themselves effectively, lead people inspirationally, and manage the non-human aspects of their organizations well - leading organisation successfully (results).

    How to do all this?
    Whatever one does to identify the naturally formed leaders should go beyond mere performance assessment, delving into the authentic approach to their leadership - how they truly lead on the field. Once identified, these natural leaders should receive the recognition and support they deserve, resulting in collective affirmation of their successful leadership, rather than individual declaration.

    Awards and Beyond
    Once identified these leaders should be celebrated with an exclusive Successful Leadership Award, signaling not just achievement but the potential to lead at a higher and deeper level, authentically, and aligned to the organization’s culture and needs. So the commitment should not just be to recognize but to catalyze the further growth of these leaders, refining and leveraging their natural gifts as they shape the future of leadership.

    In a world seeking authentic leadership, we need to nurture, empower, let loose those naturally inclined towards leadership success.     The Consequences of Ignoring Real-Time Feedback in a Real-Time World

    In today's fast-paced and dynamic world, the importance of real-time feedback on a leaders daily behaviours and business performances cannot be overstated. Yet, many leaders continue to overlook this critical aspect of their development. By failing to embrace real-time feedback, leaders risk detrimental consequences that hinder their personal growth, as well as the success and longevity of their organizations. This article explores the far-reaching consequences of leaders neglecting real-time feedback and highlights the urgent need for its adoption in a real-time world.

    1. Stagnation and Missed Opportunities: Leaders who shy away from real-time feedback deny themselves the opportunity for continuous improvement. Without timely insights and constructive criticism, they become stagnant in their development, relying on outdated strategies and approaches. This lack of growth limits their ability to seize new opportunities, adapt to change, and navigate the ever-evolving business landscape.

    2. Ineffective Decision-Making: Effective decision-making is at the core of successful leadership. However, without real-time feedback, leaders are deprived of the necessary information to make informed choices. They risk making decisions based on assumptions and outdated data, leading to poor outcomes and missed potential. Real-time feedback provides valuable insights and fresh perspectives that enhance decision-making capabilities and drive positive results.

    3. Disengaged Teams and Low Morale: When leaders neglect real-time feedback, they inadvertently create an environment of limited communication and reduced trust. This lack of feedback channels stifles open dialogue and inhibits the growth and development of their teams. Without regular feedback, team members feel undervalued and disconnected, leading to disengagement, low morale, and decreased productivity. Real-time feedback fosters a culture of transparency, collaboration, and continuous improvement, nurturing motivated and high-performing teams.

    4. Inability to Address Blind Spots: Every leader has blind spots - areas of weakness or areas they are unaware of. Without real-time feedback, leaders remain oblivious to these blind spots, impeding their personal and professional growth. Failure to address these blind spots limits their potential, hinders effective leadership, and leaves them vulnerable to making critical mistakes. Real-time feedback shines a light on these blind spots, allowing leaders to proactively address them and become well-rounded and impactful leaders.

    5. Missed Opportunities for Innovation: Innovation is a key driver of success in today's competitive landscape. Leaders who disregard real-time feedback miss out on valuable insights and perspectives that can spark innovation within their organizations. Real-time feedback provides a pulse on the needs and expectations of customers, employees, and stakeholders, enabling leaders to identify gaps, adapt strategies, and foster a culture of innovation.

    Conclusion: The consequences of leaders neglecting real-time feedback on themselves and everyday performances are far-reaching and detrimental to their own growth and the success of their organizations. To thrive in a real-time world, leaders must embrace the power of timely insights, constructive criticism, and continuous improvement. By adopting real-time feedback practices, leaders can unlock their full potential, drive innovation, engage their teams, and make informed decisions that lead to long-term success.
